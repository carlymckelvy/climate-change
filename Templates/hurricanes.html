Atlantic Storm Events

Storm Events Over Time



Data from 1851 through 2015 was used to determine if there was an increase in the number of storms in the Atlantic Ocean over time as well as what types of storms were occurring to see if any trends appeared.

Looking at the chart, there does appear to be an increase in storms in general, and more damaging storms seem to be increasing as well. It's a little deceiving for the last plot point, however, since it only encompasses six years of data instead of a full decade. Tropical depression level storms seemed to spike in the second half of the 20th Century. But as those started to decline, other storm types seem to increase. So it may be that just more tropical depressions turned into more intense storms than in the past. Tropical storms, having some peaks and valleys, appear to still be increasing over time. Even though the various categories of hurricanes have appeared throughout the timeline, the last two decades seem to have a higher combination of the more intense storms than previous decades.

It has also been suggested that the intensity of a storm has increased at a faster pace, or time interval, than in the past. That would be a good next topic for analysis.
