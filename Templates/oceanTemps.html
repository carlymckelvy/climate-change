Ocean Temps

The graph shows the change in the average global sea surface temperature. Surface temperature measures the upper few meters of the ocean, but a recent study published in the journal Geophysical Research Letters shows the deep ocean is also affected. The ocean absorbs heat and is most likely increasing due to an increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere and one third of the excess heat is absorbed 700 m below the sea surface. Over the last 100 years the temperature has been increasing by about .13 degree Celsius. A hurricane’s wind speed changes based on the temperature of the water below. Less than one degree Celsius can increase a hurricane’s windspeed by 15 to 20 miles per hour which can shift a storm into a higher category of severity.
